How to Pump Gas at a Gas Station

By Muna Atiyah


Step 1: Turn Off the Engine

Park your vehicle at the gas pump and ensure that your engine is completely turned off. This includes turning off the ignition and not idling the engine. This step is crucial for safety to prevent any ignition sources near gasoline.

Image Credit

Step 2: Open the Fuel Door

Inside your vehicle, locate the lever, button, or switch that opens the fuel door. It's typically found on the driver's side, either on the floor near the driver's seat or on the side panel of the driver's door. Pop open the fuel door to access the fuel tank.

Image Credit

Step 3: Select the Fuel Type

Examine the labels on the gas pump to determine the type of fuel your vehicle requires. The labels will specify whether it's regular, premium, diesel, or another type of fuel. Ensure you select the correct fuel type to avoid engine damage.

Image Credit

Step 4: Insert Payment Method

Approach the payment terminal on the gas pump or go inside the gas station's store to pay the attendant. If you're using the pump terminal, follow these steps: - Insert your credit or debit card into the card reader. - Follow the on-screen prompts to authorize the payment. - Wait for the authorization to be confirmed.

Image Credit

Step 5: Begin Pumping Gas

Once payment is authorized, remove the gas pump nozzle from its holder and insert it into your vehicle's gas tank. Squeeze the handle to start dispensing fuel. Some pumps have a latch that allows you to lock it in place, so you don't have to hold it manually.

Image Credit

Step 6: Fill the Tank and Replace the Nozzle

Keep an eye on the fuel gauge on your dashboard. As you fill the tank, it will gradually move towards the "Full" mark. Be attentive to avoid overfilling, as most pumps will automatically stop when the tank is full. When the pump stops, gently lift the nozzle out of the tank to prevent spills. Replace the fuel cap and close the fuel door securely.

Image Credit

Step 7: Take the Receipt

If you need a receipt for your records or reimbursement, look for the option to print it on the gas pump's display. Many pumps offer this feature. If you choose to get a receipt, take it with you.
